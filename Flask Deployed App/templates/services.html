Best Practices for Farming & Plant Disease Control

  • Practice crop rotation to prevent soil-borne diseases.
  • Use certified, disease-free seeds and planting material.
  • Maintain proper plant spacing for good air circulation.
  • Regularly inspect crops for early signs of disease or pests.
  • Remove and destroy infected plants promptly to prevent spread.
  • Keep tools and equipment clean and sanitized.
  • Apply organic or recommended chemical treatments responsibly.
  • Encourage beneficial insects and natural predators in your farm ecosystem.
  • Stay informed about local disease outbreaks and prevention strategies.
